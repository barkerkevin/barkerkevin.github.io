    The White Cat Intervention

 'You will always be lucky if you know how to make friends with strange cats.'--Colonial American proverb.
     
 “The Grail is the womb of thee Beloved.” --Robert Anton Wilson

by erica snowlake

     This eerie tale takes place in 1986, while visiting my mother in Gloucester, Massachusetts, a quaint fishing town north of Boston, known for its widespread faith in, and reverence for, the Virgin Mary. Verily, one suspects an avowed cult of the Blessed V exists for the proliferation of Her Image in lawn shrines and stone grottos. Atop Good Harbor Church She holds (not baby Jesus!) but a sailing vessel in her arms, ensuring the safe return of the town’s Italian and Portuguese fishermen. Testament to the rough Atlantic winter seas, monuments along the Boulevard list hundreds of Souls resting in Davy Jone’s locker. Gloucester is on the map since the harrowing tragedy of The Perfect Storm, and for a mid-nineties, high-school, teenage-pregnancy pact. Ave Maria!(genuflect here three times).
    Nearby thrives the renowned, be-witched town of Salem. 1692 Puritan finger-pointing, land-grubbing, and Indian War hysteria erupted in charges accusing over two hundred men and

women of witchcraft, resulting in the death sentences of nineteen New Englanders (by hanging for the most part, with one poor Soul, Gilles Cory, being pressed to death with stones). A statue of Nathaniel Hawthorne peers icily at the inquisitive as modern witch Laurie Cabot holds court. At last visit I counted fifty shops catering to the occult, and for good measure - Salem holds the largest, pagan, All Hallow’s Eve costumed street party in the country. New England reeks of Lovecraftian cthulhu, while oozing Edgar Allen Poe-caliber macabre. Headless horsemen lurk amid shacks of reclusive Tea Party refugees. Gloucester folk will spin you the sad tale of Dogtown, a village wholly consumed by the Devil, its scanty population of fish widows and stray dogs gone completely mad…………
       As usual, I shebang into the Strange upon arrival. My mother lived a stone's throw from Heaven and Earth, a crystal shop and metaphysical bookstore operated by devotees of……an extra-terrestrial rock. Specifically; the gem-quality, translucent, emerald-green tektite Moldavite which fell to Earth 14.8 million years ago, shattering along the Moldau River in the Czech Bohemian plateau. Infused with specialized properties,(pertaining to its mysterious origin and travel through Space), it is theorized to be a form of obsidian meteorite ejected by a lunar volcano, whose force of impact explosion, 6 trillion megatons, reversed the Earth’s magnetic poles. Amulets of Moldavite were found buried with the oldest-known fertility cult Goddess, the Venus of Willendorf.
      The owners of Heaven and Earth happen to be caretakers of a vast private collection of Moldavite and its main U.S. distributers, clearly having invoked poetic license in classifying it “the Stone of the Holy Grail.“ The shop is filled to the brim with the raw and faceted gemstones in a variety of finely-crafted jewelry, sculpture, art, and most significantly, silver and gold Moldavite-inlaid Chalices - the Grails. The vibrational qualities inside the “temple” were palpable, and the folks in attendance, (employees or cult members?), appeared strangely trans-dimensional as well.
    True Seekers of their Craft…… thrilled by anything inter-stellar, I bought a thumbnail chunk of Moldavite, amused as it bore resemblance to the profile of a Mayan God, with an ear of corn for an ear (plus it perfectly fit in my ear!). I also purchased a book (The Keys Of Enoch by J.J. Hurtak), cover inscribed with gold-capped, rainbow pyramids, wherein the Archangel Melchizedek gifted particulars on the electromagnetic grid surrounding the Earth, with detailed locations of natural, and manmade vortices in degrees longitude and latitude! Thrilled to explore Gea’s treasure map, I returned to my Mother’s attic. My reading culminated in a purr-fect afternoon catnap. We had plans to attend a Disney film, The Flight of the Navigator, after dinner (my dearheart, Helen, Goddess Bless Her, humors the off-kilter wackiness we share).

      As we head out that night, a scampering flash catches my eye on the sidewalk, illuminated by a spectral Full Moon. Holy of Holies! The White Cat!!! : a fetish / totem animal symbolizing the Supreme Powers of Enchantment, Purity, and Good Fortune, in cultural mythologies worldwide, synonymous with Wonderland’s White Rabbit - Totemic Guardians of Secret Portals beyond Time, Space, and Magician hats. White Cats specifically represent the Joy of Immortality (and the Immortality of Joy!) for the enchanted, entheogenic aficionado (especially beloved by the Norse Goddess Freya, everyone is familiar with the Japanese “maneki neko”, the ubiquitous “beckoning cat” (with one paw raised)). Naturally I am being called to worship, leaving Mom across the street, dismayed, hands on hips, biting her tongue. The cat’s back arches with affection as I caper alongside it……and go slack-jawed, as two willowy beings, wearing hooded robes, manifest in front of me. I enter into a trance, subconsciously registering a chilling fact: their shadowy features are indistinguishable from hallowed, empty space. Cloaking devices? Telepathic (hypnotic) transmissions reveal they are, indeed, my true brethren…..whom, naturally, I am invited to accompany, with whatever Flesh they possess. Or lack there of. No matter….they possess my own. The White Cat / Extra-Terrestrial Ambassador leads the Way. I begin to follow these excellent strangers, totally oblivious my mother is witnessing my odd behavior from afar.      
       A familiar, maternal, “STOP! COME OVER HERE NOW!” rings out with panic-laced Authority. I choose to rebel, resenting her selfish reasons to bust my destiny. I’m about to ditch her in my bewitchment, but her superpowers (boosted by the Virgin) intervene to break my spell. It’s not my fate to be led away by these epic good folk to pagan LaLaLand after all…(reality letdown, vacuum-sucking paralysis, mind-yawning regret)….and so concluded the imminent threat (and the present narrative). The Beings faded into NOTHINGNESS, as quickly as they had appeared. I returned to Helen’s side, and we were both left wondering “What the hell just happened? and who in Heaven’s name were they?” To this day,I am still attempting to cognate the fact THEY HAD NO ASCERTAINABLE FACES! Justifying her harsh intervention, my mother felt I was about to be harmfully, albeit willingly, abducted (and we’ll never know). When we arrived at the theater, the movie had been cancelled, the night ended; curtain drawn, ceremony hidden….. unbidden, the sacrifice remained alive?
       Years later, I rented the The Flight of the Navigator. In a weird twist of fate, I know the actor who plays the kid in the movie. He grew up in the rural community I now live in (and coincidentally, makes sci-fi props for films). The film’s synthesized soundtrack is classic, cheesy eighties and the vicarious fun watching twelve-year-old Joey Cramer navigate the silver pod is irresistible, even with Paul (Pee-wee Herman) Reubens’ insidious voice at the alien helm. It’s Twilight Zone strange, the storyline is about time travel and a UFO abduction (“In 1978 a kid is moved 8 years into the future…”). Stranger still, as I do not drive, and have never owned a car (after a brief, disastrous career), I stave off queries of folks who find it odd, declaring : ”I’m saving my skills for a spaceship, and a road with no lines.”
