The wee folk strained under a usurious tax on crossings of Blazewater Bay, imposed by the Crystal Duchess of the Rainy Isle , and dark muttering was heard from every corner of the kingdom

heon returned from the foreign wars to find a different world. The streets of Ling’s Landing were quiet except for the clank and ever-present hum of machines.

The elvish kings who reigned in starry hollow had been dethroned by the Moorlocks who had overwhelmed their borders and scattered or pressed them into servitude; their culture systematically destroyed. The week-long solstice festivities, the parade of elaborately costumed exotic animals and glittering show of arms, and the feasting and music which followed had been absorbed into a monoculture. The sacred herbs that sustained the visions of the high priests were eradicated. The elvish realm was put to flight.


The Moorlocks and their neighbours, the Storks at Winteros across the waters to the north, had chosen pharaohs as their leaders, (the Winteros mantle had fallen to the Venerable Mosfora), and they built Great Works as symbols of their supremacy and power. The elves, the dwarves, the groundlings were forced to labour on them as tribute, and woe to those who shirked. The Moorlock King, Robert, scion of the great warrior Handinstone, was guarded by cowled wizards who criss-crossed the land in their carriages of black, their faces barely discernible behind screened windows, and the populace hid from them for they were the eyes and ears of the great sheriff and vizier, Richard Glassbottom.  To impress the common people the boy king had brought back heraldry. The silver argent on his carriage door held the unmistakeable likeness of the fabled oriental king Ozymandias whose marble tomb was one of the seven wonders. It was said the architect had been slain to prevent duplication. It bore no name, or at least none that could be found; merely an inscription which read, “If anyone would know how great I am and where I lie, let him surpass one of my works.” Handinstone had embarked on a similar campaign, a Great Tomb to rival the masterpieces of the ancient world. But there was trouble in the highest circles of Moorlock rule. The young Robert had alienated the high commissars with his capricious ways. The location of the Great Tomb kept changing, and it was muttered that the boy king wanted its location kept permanently secret: Some had been banished and some fled, while others steadfastly refused to work on it.

For his part, Mosfera of the northern Storks had constructed at great public cost a marvelous circus maximus to appease his subjects, though it was so grand that few dared to use it; and on the Iron Islands across Blazewater Bay a great publik toilette was erected in honor of the Lord Mayor John Nuttor, at a cost of one hundred thousand ducats.  The toilette measured a breathtaking 100 by 80 by 60 cubits, and was the most wonderful public convenience of all the realms. Here too there was grumbling. Once prosperous throughout the land, the proud middle classes were buckling under an onerous tax burden. Many opposed lavishing the public purse on such extravagances. But their protests came to naught, and in their diminished state they were simply referred to by the pharaohs as ‘the small folk’.

What remained were the wildings beyond the wall, pushed further back into the hinterlands, across the fjords and inlets of the fractured coastline which stretched to the Islands of Always Winter on the Bay of Lee some five hundred leagues hence; and the mother of dragons, the iconic elf whose ringletted hair was said to be spun from gold. She had fled with the Jade Sorceress to dwell among the dwarves; the woodcutters and farmers who tilled the land and worshipped the trees as their elders. The Dwarf King lived in seclusion, and passed the long winter nights constructing hideous wooden masks which evoked the dream time when the dieties of the old world roamed the woods at will and croaked with the bullfrogs. The Farmers of Gate continued their agrarian ways, but hidden now, and they took great pains to hide their bounty from the penetrating eyes of the vizier Glassbottom. The Moorlock had encroached here too, hacking and burning and hauling the great trees as tribute to the Black Sorcerer of the West Bloc and his acolyte, the Crystal Duchess of the Rainy Isle.

There was a far greater prize in the realm than the Elder Trees. The ground of the Eyrie lands to the northeast was said to hold a magical black elixer, a talismán that conjured great wealth and power, enough to enslave the world beyond Storm’s End and the Shivering Sea. It was prized above all else by the Black Sorcerer and the Crystal Duchess, and alchemists had been despached to study its extraction and use.

It It was here, in the hills above Starry hollow, that Theon took refuge, together with the mother of dragons and the jade sorceress…
