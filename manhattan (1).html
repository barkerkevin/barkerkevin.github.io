Excerpted from 'Suck the New Flesh - A Pyrotechnical Manifesto', by Erica Snowlake

 

    As pertains bad faeries, (and you know who you are), this incident occurred on the rooftop of the fabled, six-storied, Manhattan nightclub, the 21st Street Danceteria. Early eighties. After snorting heroin, (or some reputed debauchery), at my dear friend P’s in Alphabetland (Tompkins Square Park, home of the beautiful and reckless sinnin’ Saints Alive), we sped across the night,tracing the glossy black sidewalks flecked-with-gold. My, how they shine! One could possibly live in their sparkling dimensions. Danceteria. Past midnight. Do I hallucinate Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds? Too bad, big fan, gotta go… am being summoned to the roof…. coming to attentive command-reality in the service of a Psychokiller♪ / Kathy Acker wannabe (in retrospect, I know it was her, the tattooed, cut-up artist / writer / punk poet-in-the-flesh, the title of whom’s debut novel, The Childlike Life of the Black Tarantula: Some Lives of Murderesses, erases all doubt). Between cigarette flicks, she nukes my high with malevolent vibes, using me like I possessed the Suicide Kid, as show-off, silly putty for her friends. She zones inside my head like a missile target, brain-warping icky, zig-zag commandos; specifically : “JUMP OFF THE ROOF!”
     She arrests me without reading my rights, and with absolutely no dialogue; using pure, electrified transmission, and focused, nasty intent. “Don’t push me cause I’m close to the edge.”♪ Being straight, (i.e. not on anything), in the Big Apple, is, by osmosis, already tripping. NYC oozes Saint Anthony’s Fire and Saint Vitus Bedlam Dance combined (i faint the moment I arrive, praying to Tesla for salvation). Now some freak attack bat’s using the grid to blackout my brain, whaaammmy of a migraine! Challenging me to psychically duel, I’m facing a life or death situation on the roof of Danceteria, of all places. How powerfully amazing! Hello New York Minute! She would have won, if I engaged at all, and she would have won twice, by a) forcing me to stoop to her abysmal level, and b) proving Schrodinger’s cat can’t play Undead-in-the-box forever. 
     I’d have been over the roof and done by, but for the grace and indomitable nature of my protective, inner-temple-sanctum / Fatima bloodline, stepping up to bat, absolving her abstracted Morticia-act deadheading the roses. The mounting, insane, B-movie laugh track echoing from her cohorts fizzles as I disengage, swaying as she rabidly sucks out a few more twinkling lights for the road, starving-alien-preying-mantis (I, Braineater). A familiar gentle giant intervenes, just-in-time, to escort me off the roof (note to Self : brush up on Dion Fortune’s Psychic Self-Defense). Chickiepoo was one badass, black hole of a mischievous pixie, some witches have no sparkles of their own, not even a fleck. Why, there’s sidewalks in New York with more light in ‘em.
