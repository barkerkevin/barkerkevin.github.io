Adam Cramb is an urban artist and poet from Powell River, B.C. who writes in a disembodied poetic style; at once lyrical and dissonate; fragmentary and only very loosely thematic. He illustrates his own work with photography and collage.

 

Teenage novella 2

by adam cramb

Or of what you are are you is all that we care of you is what they told you you would have to give when you were born and now you regret this application. To walk alone home amongst these messages . Between these teenage lines there is a contemptuous ravaged compulsion. And we rage. In this fiction are these cries lies or are they wriiten within for the others does he even think  for himself. Fiction. Fiction. Can one be sure? For these un-born messiahs, prophetless. These un-born myth ragers. Are we all too too progressive for letters. We are indeed these Devil's childrens.
Only in spirit though. Only in our spirits. Streetwalkers compounded and bound. Are you really hot(chaud) or just stupid? A post post dharma. Scribblings of poems  upon rippled cardboard scraps greased soaked, riddled. Spread aloof, scuffed and shuffled my children. You do not want this life. It can not change upon desires. A determination too quickly becomes mass solmen. A heart is breaking. For lack of a better word, un-bound. A slaughter. I am not bold not brave like massacres. A childerns youth like you do not want this life Boys. You broken boys so sun varnished and sun shine polished. Your backs.  Ravens and sisters no longer waiting. Such assumptions are desperate longings. Anxiety will tell us that your dreams can never be fufilled. The rest of your life just filler. Like the last ten years.
When your body has given its last moments. It will have given them to others. They don't want them. They need them. They don't want them. They can't wait to die. What do they demand? Never conspiratated. How passionless, how powerful. But somehow they make a life of it and make-up like sex bunnies. Waiting for themselves. Waiting on somebody's forboeding. Waiting for their next big break. Broken.
The best peice of advice I can give you is to invest years into insult without too much feeling. Leave the last parts of your purpose to despising your parents. Even  if you can't really write, eventually you will bury yourself in the wilderness. Literally consumed. You born with evrything you need to make it on your own. A biiter longing emptiness of something missing in your life. Your purpose. Unconditional words repeating themselves. Swiftly driftless abstractions, regrettable and un-reasonable. Forceless forms jostle for placement across your brow. It in this summer's figures that relay our startled movements. Words we should not know but are delivered upon our shorelines. “Be excellent to each other!”
